Privacy Policy

Effective Date: June 10, 2026

Welcome to PeekProof. Your privacy is critically important to us. We have built PeekProof from the ground up with a "privacy-first" architecture. This Privacy Policy explains what information is collected, how it is used, and how your data is protected when you use the PeekProof Chrome Extension ("the Extension").

1. Information We Collect

Short Answer: We do not collect, transmit, or store any personal data.

PeekProof operates entirely locally on your device within your Google Chrome browser. We do not have servers, databases, or analytics trackers.

Data Stored Locally

The only data the Extension saves are your UI preferences (e.g., whether the Thumbnail Shield is set to "Blur" or "Black", and which shields are toggled on or off).

This configuration data is saved exclusively using Chrome’s native chrome.storage.sync API. This means your settings are synced securely across your devices using your logged-in Google account. We do not have access to this data.

2. Permissions and Why We Need Them

To provide its core functionality, PeekProof requests the following permissions upon installation. We strictly limit the use of these permissions to the features described below:

  • <all_urls> (Read and change all your data on all websites): PeekProof’s "Pause Shield" feature automatically detects when a video is paused and covers the video frame to protect your privacy. Because videos can be embedded on any website across the internet (e.g., news sites, social media, blogs), PeekProof requires permission to monitor video elements on all websites you visit. We do not read your browsing history, intercept network requests, or read your personal data on these websites. The permission is used exclusively to find <video> tags and overlay them with a black screen when paused.
  • storage: Used solely to save your customized shield settings and synchronize them across your signed-in Chrome browsers.

3. Data Sharing and Third Parties

Because we do not collect any data, we do not sell, rent, share, or disclose any data to third parties. PeekProof does not include any third-party tracking scripts, analytics tools (such as Google Analytics), or advertising SDKs.

4. Chrome Web Store Disclosure

In compliance with the Chrome Web Store Developer Program Policies:

  • PeekProof does not handle personal or sensitive user data.
  • PeekProof does not transfer any data outside of your local browser environment.
  • All processing related to blurring thumbnails, hiding titles, and overlaying videos occurs directly in your browser's DOM (Document Object Model).

5. Children's Privacy

PeekProof does not collect personal information from anyone, including children under the age of 13.

6. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect modifications to the Extension or regulatory requirements. Any changes will be posted on this page, and the "Effective Date" at the top will be updated. We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically.

7. Contact Us

If you have any questions, concerns, or feedback regarding this Privacy Policy or how PeekProof handles privacy, please contact the developer via the support tab on the Chrome Web Store listing.
